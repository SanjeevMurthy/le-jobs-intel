## Company Overview

Palo Alto Networks, the global cybersecurity leader serving 85,000+ enterprises, is making aggressive investments across three fronts in their Bengaluru engineering center: (1) cloud-native security platform infrastructure processing trillions of events daily, (2) GenAI-powered security products including Prisma AIRS, and (3) AI-driven internal business systems for their go-to-market platform. Every role is Staff or Principal level, signaling a deliberate strategy to build a senior-heavy, high-autonomy engineering organization in India. The company is moving from AI research prototypes to production-grade, enterprise-scale GenAI security products — a pivotal transformation moment.

## Hiring Signals

- **GenAI security platform (Prisma AIRS) is a flagship initiative** — A dedicated Sr Staff ML Engineer role focused on productionizing LLM-based agents, evaluation workflows, and MLOps for a cybersecurity GenAI platform. This is not an experiment — it's a named product with serious engineering investment.
- **AI is permeating every engineering function** — 3 of 5 JDs explicitly require or prefer AI/LLM skills, including a core software role, an IT platform role, and the dedicated ML role. Even the platform engineering role lists "Generative AI (RAG, Agents)" as preferred.
- **Senior-IC-only hiring strategy** — 100% Staff/Principal hires. This signals a team that expects high autonomy, minimal management overhead, and deep technical ownership. They are not building teams — they are adding force multipliers.
- **Multi-cloud but GCP-first** — GCP appears in all 5 JDs while AWS/Azure appear in 4. The DevOps role also mentions OCI, indicating a cloud-agnostic operations strategy with GCP as the primary platform.
- **Trillions-of-events scale** — The DevOps role explicitly states infrastructure processes trillions of events daily, indicating massive-scale distributed systems that few companies operate.
- **Bengaluru as a core engineering center** — All 5 roles are in Bengaluru with 4 of 5 requiring onsite presence. This is not a satellite office — it's a primary engineering hub for core product work.
- **LLM security as a differentiator** — The IT Software Engineer role requires LLM security expertise (prompt injection prevention, input validation), and the ML role lists AI safety and adversarial ML. Palo Alto Networks is applying its security DNA to AI.
- **Open-source engagement valued** — The Platform Engineering role explicitly mentions contributing to open-source projects, signaling an engineering culture that values community participation.

## Candidate Strategy

- **Target the Staff Software Engineer (Platform) role if you have 5-9 years with Python/Go + Kubernetes** — It's the most accessible of the five roles, with the broadest skill match for generalist backend/platform engineers. GenAI (RAG, Agents) as a preferred (not required) skill gives you a differentiation opportunity.
- **The Sr Staff ML Engineer role is for specialized ML engineers ready to productionize** — Don't apply with only research/notebook experience. This role demands production MLOps, model serving at scale, and CI/CD for ML artifacts. If you've shipped LLM features to production, this is a high-impact opportunity on Prisma AIRS.
- **Principal roles require 9-15+ years and architectural ownership** — The Sr Principal Software Engineer expects 15+ years. The Principal DevOps Engineer expects 9+. Both require demonstrated leadership in system architecture and mentoring without a management title.
- **Position yourself through the cybersecurity lens** — Every project, skill, and accomplishment should be framed in terms of security impact. "Built a data pipeline" becomes "Built a petabyte-scale threat intelligence pipeline." This is a security company first.
- **In cover letters, reference PAN's scale and mission** — Mention the trillions of daily security events, 85,000+ enterprise customers, and the challenge of applying GenAI to cybersecurity. Show you understand the mission isn't just engineering — it's protecting the digital world.
- **Interview preparation focus areas:**
  - System design for multi-cloud, multi-tenant security platforms at massive scale
  - Kubernetes architecture, networking, and troubleshooting
  - LLM application design: RAG, agents, evaluation, cost optimization
  - Distributed systems: consensus, fault tolerance, event processing at scale
  - Behavioral: IC leadership, mentoring, cross-functional collaboration, ownership
  - For ML role: model serving, fine-tuning pipelines, ML observability, AI safety
  - For DevOps role: SRE practices, SLO governance, incident management, IaC at scale
